About

Research that blends engineering, perception, and embodiment.

I am a researcher in VR/AR, haptics, and robotics, with work centered on making immersive systems feel more natural, expressive, and physically meaningful. My interests include multisensory rendering, embodied interaction, mediated social touch, and interactive robotic systems for extended reality.

What I do

I design and evaluate immersive systems where sensing, control, and haptic feedback work together. That includes wearable haptic devices, real-time interaction pipelines, multimodal rendering, and user-centered experiments to understand comfort, realism, and social perception.

Haptics + XR Building interaction techniques that go beyond sight and sound.
Robotics Integrating motion, actuation, and physical interfaces into immersive systems.
User Studies Evaluating realism, comfort, perception, and social experience.
Prototyping From Unity and sensing pipelines to embedded systems and hardware control.
